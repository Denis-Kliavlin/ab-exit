# AB-EXIT: Legal Analysis for the USA

## What AB-EXIT Does NOT Block

### 14th Amendment / "One Person, One Vote"
This principle (Reynolds v. Sims, 1964; Evenwel v. Abbott, 2016) concerns the apportionment of electoral districts — each district must contain a roughly equal number of people. It does NOT require that every person actually votes. The weight enhancement of a vote in AB-EXIT is a mathematical consequence of voluntary electorate contraction, not a discriminatory legal mechanism.

### 24th Amendment (Poll Tax)
AB-EXIT is not a tax on voting. It is compensation for not voting. Legally, these are distinct concepts. A poll tax prohibits PAYING for the right to vote. AB-EXIT PAYS for the refusal to vote.

## Real Barrier: 18 U.S.C. § 597

Federal law prohibits paying for voting or refusing to vote. HOWEVER: this applies ONLY to federal elections (President, Senate, Congress).

**State-level Firewall:** The 10th Amendment of the Constitution leaves the conduct of state elections to the discretion of the states. The AB-EXIT referendum text is restricted to elections WITHOUT federal candidates on the ballot.

**Likelihood of surviving court challenges: 55-65%.**

## 6 Levels of Attack and Defense

### Level 1: § 597
- **Attack:** The DOJ blocks the referendum.
- **Defense:** State-level Firewall. Only state elections are involved. Federal authorities lack jurisdiction.

### Level 2: Voting Rights Act / Disparate Impact
- **Attack:** The ACLU alleges discrimination against the poor and minorities.
- **Defense:** Voluntariness + a floating rate tied to the median income (disparate impact disappears mathematically).

### Level 3: Financial Strangling (Operation Choke Point)
- **Attack:** Banks/PayPal close project accounts.
- **Defense:** DAO with a crypto-treasury managed via multi-sig wallets.

### Level 4: Information Blockade (Big Tech)
- **Attack:** YouTube adds warning labels, Google deprioritizes search results.
- **Defense:** IPFS/Arweave + the Streisand effect.

### Level 5: State Constitutional Challenges
- **Attack:** The constitution of a specific state limits such reform.
- **Defense:** Retaining state-specific constitutional lawyers PRIOR to signature collection.

### Level 6: Practical Implementation
- **Attack:** "It's unrealizable; who will administer it?"
- **Defense:** Technical solutions embedded in the text of the law (election commission, registry, automatic transfers, autonomous trust fund).

## Endgame: SCOTUS

The current composition of SCOTUS is the most pro-state in decades. Precedents include:
- *Dobbs v. Jackson* (2022): Return of powers to the states.
- *West Virginia v. EPA* (2022): Limitation of federal agency authority.
- The weakening of the *Chevron* doctrine.
