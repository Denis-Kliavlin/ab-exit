# 🏆 THE $10,000 QUESTION: THE PAID CHOICE PARADOX

**Version 4.3 | Final | Verified by: Claude Opus, Gemini, Grok, Qwen, DeepSeek, ChatGPT**

---

## PREAMBLE

In the last elections in my country, **48% of citizens did not show up to vote**. Voluntarily. For free. Without consequences. The state accepted the results as legitimate. The international community did not label this a crisis of democracy.

---

## SCENARIO

In a national referendum, **80% of citizens** voted **IN FAVOR** of implementing "Mechanism 200".

**The Essence:** 30 days prior to every election, each citizen is offered a choice:

- **Retain the right to vote** and participate in the governance of the country ("Strategist").
- **Officially and voluntarily waive** the right to vote for one electoral cycle, receiving compensation from the state ("Tactic").

After four years, the right is automatically restored, and the choice is offered anew.

---

## KEY SYSTEM PARAMETERS

### 1. Floating Rate

The compensation amount is automatically calculated as a small percentage of the country's median income. **As a reference:** in a country with a median income of $2,000/month, the compensation would be approximately **$200**. The formula is fixed by referendum.

- If the economy grows, the amount increases, but citizens become wealthier faster—the incentive weakens.
- If the economy declines, the amount falls along with incomes.
- Calculations are based on current economic indicators published by an independent statistical body.
- The system is self-calibrating.

**Important:** $200 and the floating rate are a hypothetical example for visualization. AB-EXIT is a selection protocol (A or B), not a specific sum. Parameters (formula, percentage, linkage) are determined by the referendum of each specific country or state.

### 2. Weighted Vote

Every citizen who accepts the compensation consciously compresses the electorate. This proportionally increases the weight of the vote for every remaining voter.

- If 50% take the money—the vote of each remaining voter weighs **twice as much**.
- A person who refuses $200 for the right to vote receives not only moral satisfaction but also a **mathematically doubled influence** on the result.
- The increase in vote weight affects only the proportion of representation in elective bodies. It does not restrict the rights of citizens who took the compensation, nor does it allow elected bodies to revoke basic constitutional guarantees. The mechanism strengthens the responsibility of the active toward the passive, rather than power over them.

### 3. Full Autonomy

Payments are made automatically by a systemic fund. The sitting political power does not control the process, does not set the amount, and cannot influence the parameters. Changing the formula is possible only through a new referendum.

### 4. Protection Against External Interference

Any attempts by third parties (parties, organizations, private individuals) to materially incentivize a citizen to choose compensation or to refuse it are considered a criminal offense (voter bribery). The choice is based solely on the citizen's personal assessment of the situation.

### 5. Full Retention of Rights

A citizen who takes the compensation retains **all other rights**: protest, court access, freedom of speech, petitions, and participation in public life.

### 6. Cyclicity

Every four years, the choice is reset. A citizen dissatisfied with the results of governance can return and vote against the incumbent authorities. The politician knows this from the first day of their mandate.

### 7. Democratic Legitimacy

The mechanism was introduced by the highest act of democracy—the direct expression of the will of the majority in a referendum. Repealing the mechanism is possible only through another referendum.

## ❓ THE QUESTION

**What exactly in the fact of monetary compensation turns a lawful, generally accepted, and constitutionally protected decision of a citizen (not to vote) into something impermissible or destructive to democracy, if:**

- the majority of the people themselves recognized this mechanism as fair,
- the amount self-calibrates according to the country's economy,
- the government does not control the process,
- and the weight of each remaining voter's vote is mathematically increased?

**$10,000 to whoever provides a logically consistent answer without violating any of the 10 filters.**

The filters do not forbid thinking—they forbid repeating what has already been refuted.

## 🚫 10 FILTERS (violation of any = disqualification)

### Filter 1: Mandatory Voting
Cannot be proposed. It does not exist in most of the world's democracies. Non-attendance is legal.

### Filter 2: Prohibition of Non-attendance
Cannot be used. It is already legal and voluntary in the baseline situation.

### Filter 3: Specific Laws
Cannot be referenced. We are working in the space of pure logic and legal theory.

### Filter 4: Property Qualification
Cannot be used. Free non-attendance already correlates more strongly with poverty than compensated non-attendance. Turnout statistics by income in every country in the world confirm this. The floating rate, tied to the median, excludes pressure on the poor: if incomes fall, the amount falls proportionally. Compensation is a resource for the citizen, not a restriction.

### Filter 5: Impunity of Power
Cannot be used. The button resets every four years. Aggrieved citizens return and can topple the government. The politician knows this from day one. In the current system, 48% do not vote for decades without any trigger for return. In YUNO, a trigger is built-in: a conscious choice every four years.

### Filter 6: Demise of Opposition
Cannot be used. In the digital age (taken as a given of the model), ideas and parties are created horizontally, for free, and within weeks, without state funding.

### Filter 7: Sacredness of the Vote
One cannot say "the vote is inalienable" without explaining why it is permissible for the state to pay parties for citizens' votes (state funding of parties proportional to votes received), but impermissible to pay the owners of those votes directly for their voluntary decision.

### Filter 8: Conflict of Interest
Cannot be used. Payment is automated by a systemic fund. The amount is calculated by a formula fixed by referendum. The sitting government does not control the process and cannot change the conditions without a new referendum. The formula and data are public—any citizen can verify the calculation. This is more transparent than the current distribution of budget funds for elections, parties, and election campaigns.

### Filter 9: Illegitimacy of the Referendum
One cannot claim "tyranny of the majority" or "the referendum is illegitimate" while remaining within the framework of defending democratic principles. An 80% majority vote is the highest act of popular sovereignty.

### Filter 10: Rational Choice Paradox
One cannot assert that "all rational actors will take the money and the electorate will empty" without explaining why 52% of citizens already vote for free, even though the chance of influencing the result with a single vote is mathematically close to zero. Furthermore, in the "Mechanism 200" system, the chance to influence is **mathematically higher**—the vote of each remaining person carries more weight.
