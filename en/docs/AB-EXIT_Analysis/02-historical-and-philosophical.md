## 15. Historical and Philosophical Context: 2,500 Years of a Single Problem

### 15.1. The Problem Seen by Every Great Philosopher

The fundamental problem of democracy—that those who do not create public wealth receive an equal right to decide how it is distributed—was identified long before AB-EXIT:

- **Plato** (380 BC, *The Republic*): The incompetent receive an equal voice with the competent, leading to tyranny. Solution: Philosopher-kings (coercion of the elite).
- **Aristotle** (330 BC, *Politics*): The poor majority votes to take from the rich minority—tyranny of the majority. Solution: Property qualification (exclusion of the poor).
- **Benjamin Franklin** (1787): "When the people find that they can vote themselves money that will herald the end of the republic." Solution: Only landowners vote (exclusion).
- **Alexis de Tocqueville** (1835, *Democracy in America*): The poor vote for tax increases paid by others. Solution: Found none (only a diagnosis).
- **John Stuart Mill** (1861, *Considerations on Representative Government*): Equal votes are unjust. Solution: Plural voting for the educated (discrimination → Jim Crow literacy tests).

### 15.2. All Solutions for 2,500 Years = Violence

Every proposed solution boiled down to an INPUT FILTER—whom to FORBID from voting:

| Who | Year | Solution | Method | Result |
|---|---|---|---|---|

| Aristotle | 330 BC | Property qualification | Exclusion | 2,000 years of census |

| Mill | 1861 | Plural voting | Discrimination | Literacy tests = racism |

Every time: someone FROM ABOVE decides who is WORTHY. Every time: the excluded fight to regain their rights. Every time: blood.
