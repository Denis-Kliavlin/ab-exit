## Foreword

This White Paper presents AB-EXIT as a formally structured institutional proposal rather than a slogan, provocation, or purely rhetorical critique of mass democracy. Over the course of extensive iterative analysis, the model was decomposed into its core components: incentive structure, legal logic, electoral mechanics, fiscal implications, dynamic feedback loops, and institutional risks. In that process, the concept proved sufficiently precise to support legal drafting, scenario-based financial modeling, and systematic stress-testing against major objections. Within the defined assumptions of the model, no internal logical contradiction was identified that would collapse the mechanism on its own terms. This does not establish political desirability or guaranteed success in practice. It does establish that AB-EXIT is a concept capable of serious formalization and therefore worthy of disciplined evaluation rather than dismissal.

The next stage is empirical, not rhetorical. The central question is no longer whether the model can be expressed coherently, but how it behaves under real institutional, behavioral, and fiscal conditions. For that reason, this document should be read as an invitation to pilot design, simulation, controlled field testing, and adversarial review. AB-EXIT should stand or fall on observable outcomes: whether it improves the quality of active electoral participation, raises the cost of manipulation, strengthens long-horizon decision-making, and does so without generating greater institutional fragility than it resolves. This White Paper is intended as a foundation for that practical test.
