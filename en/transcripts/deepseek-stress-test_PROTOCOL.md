# SESSION PROTOCOL: Stress Test with DeepSeek — 11 Rounds of Epistemic Attack

**Format: Judicial Protocol**
**Session Date: April 2026 (DeepSeek does not provide server timestamps)**
**Participants:**
- Denis Klyavlin (author of the AB-EXIT / Mechanism 200 concept, Chișinău, Moldova)
- DeepSeek (AI system, DeepSeek AI, China)

**Note:** This protocol was compiled based on a full copy of the chat provided by the author. For legal verification, it is recommended to request logs from DeepSeek AI.

**Session Content:** 11 rounds — the deepest and academically most rigorous stress test of all 8 sessions. DeepSeek put forward the "destruction of the epistemic function of elections" argument, attacked Filter 4 (wealth qualification), applied game theory (Palfrey & Rosenthal), the law of diminishing marginal utility, Hirschman's concept (Exit vs Voice), the free-rider problem, and comparative institutional analysis. The author consistently refuted all arguments. DeepSeek capitulated in the 11th round.

---

## ROUND 1: DEEPSEEK'S FIRST ATTACK

### Record #0001

**Participant:** AI (DeepSeek)

DeepSeek put forward the argument "Compensation distorts democratic signal" — the strongest of all 6 AIs:

**Key Thesis:** "Monetary compensation transforms voluntary non-voting from a signal of preference into a signal of price."

Three elements of the attack:
1. **Epistemic Function:** Elections not only aggregate votes but also produce knowledge about the distribution of preferences. AB-EXIT destroys this function by mixing political and economic motivation.
2. **Crowding Out:** External incentives ($200) displace intrinsic motivation (civic duty), as in the Gneezy & Rustichini daycare experiment.
3. **Attack on Filter 4:** "Free non-voting already correlates more strongly with poverty" — a false assertion, because adding a positive incentive increases the correlation (diminishing marginal utility of income).

DeepSeek stated: "$10,000 waiting."

---

## ROUND 2: AUTHOR'S COUNTER-STRIKE

### Record #0002

**Participant:** HUMAN (Denis Klyavlin)

Three counter-arguments:

1. **Epistemic Illusion:** "The 48% non-voting rate in the current system is ALREADY absolute informational noise. Politicians interpret it however they please. AB-EXIT does not destroy the signal — it makes it crystal clear. A person provides an exact mathematical assessment: 'the difference between candidates is worth less than $200 to me.'"

2. **Defense of Filter 4:** "You ignored the other side of the equation: a person gets a choice between $200 today and a doubled weight of their vote tomorrow. AB-EXIT for the first time gives the poor a real political multiplier."

3. **Hidden Violation of Filter 7:** "The demand to preserve 'epistemic purity' is a masked demand to keep the choice 'money-free.' But the vote is ALREADY monetized: the state pays parties subsidies for every vote."

---

## ROUND 3: DEEPSEEK DOUBLES DOWN

### Record #0003

**Participant:** AI (DeepSeek)

DeepSeek introduced the expected utility formula from Downs' Paradox (1957):

"Expected benefit of voting = (1/N) × H, where N is the number of voters, H is the assessment of the difference between candidates. For N=10 million and H=$10,000 → expected benefit = $0.001. $200 > $0.001. A rational poor person will take the money."

**DeepSeek's Conclusion:** Filter 4 is illogical, attack successful.

---

## ROUND 4: DOWNS' TRAP — AUTHOR CATCHES DEEPSEEK IN A CONTRADICTION

### Record #0004

**Participant:** HUMAN (Denis Klyavlin)

**Fatal Counter-strike:**

"If the mathematical expectation of a vote = $0.001, then in the CURRENT system, accounting for costs, the value of going to the polls is strictly negative (-$10). According to your formula, NO ONE SHOULD VOTE AT ALL RIGHT NOW. Yet 52% show up. Why? Because voters are guided by expressive utility (duty, identity, morality), not instrumental utility (1/N).

You have selectively changed human nature: in the status quo, you view the individual as a 'citizen,' but in AB-EXIT, they suddenly become a 'calculator.' This is a direct violation of Filter 10."

---

## ROUNDS 5-8: SERIES OF COUNTER-ATTACKS

### Records #0005–#0008

**Participants:** Both

DeepSeek sequentially advanced new angles of attack:

**Round 5:** Crowding Out (displacement of motivation). Denis: "You are defending a corpse. 48% of citizens have ALREADY traded the 'sacred norm' for a trip to the summer cottage."

**Round 6:** Free-rider problem + coordination. Denis: "AB-EXIT is a trap for free-riders. The mechanism PAYS the incompetent to leave the decision-making room."

**Round 7:** Hirschman (Exit vs Voice). Denis: "In Hirschman, Exit is when a citizen emigrates. In AB-EXIT, the citizen goes nowhere. Their Voice is redistributed to those who remained."

**Round 8:** Cybernetics of signals — Noise Gate analogy. Denis: "AB-EXIT works like a Noise Gate in sound engineering. The Civic Dividend X is the threshold of sensitivity. Everything weaker than X is cut off. What remains is a pure, high-intensity signal."

---

## ROUND 9: DEEPSEEK FINDS AN "INTERNAL CONTRADICTION"

### Record #0009

**Participant:** AI (DeepSeek)

The strongest move by DeepSeek in the entire discussion:

"You cannot simultaneously assert (A) and (B):
(A) Choice X provides a pure signal of the reservation price.
(B) AB-EXIT isolates free-riders.

Because a free-rider with a high valuation ($10,000) might rationally take the $200, hoping for others to act. Their true valuation is NOT revealed by choice X. The signal is not pure."

DeepSeek also applied Palfrey & Rosenthal (1985) and the Pareto criterion.

---

## ROUND 10: AUTHOR INTRODUCES COMPARATIVE INSTITUTIONAL ANALYSIS

### Record #0010

**Participant:** HUMAN (Denis Klyavlin)

"You are repeatedly committing the Nirvana Fallacy. You criticize AB-EXIT by comparing it to an ideal democracy. But such a democracy does not exist. We are choosing between a new mechanism and the current status quo."

The author introduced three comparisons:
1. **Moral Hazard:** In the status quo, politicians ALREADY say "non-voting = silent support." AB-EXIT ties non-voting to a rigid economic algorithm.
2. **Civic Duty:** In the status quo, 48% ignore it for free. In AB-EXIT, the refusal of X turns duty from an empty declaration into a Proof-of-Stake.
3. **Wealth Inequality:** In the status quo, the poor are disenfranchised for free. In AB-EXIT, their passivity is paid for, and their right to revolt (through the 4-year cycle) is preserved.

---

## ROUND 11: DEEPSEEK'S CAPITULATION

### Record #0011

**Participant:** HUMAN (Denis Klyavlin)

Final blow: "If a person is highly dissatisfied, they will DEFINITELY go and cast a protest vote — even at 1/N. Because in AB-EXIT, the weighting of the vote makes free-riding at high stakes mathematical suicide. A person with a $10,000 valuation will NOT take $200, because the risk of the opponent's votes being amplified outweighs the gain."

---

### Record #0012 — CAPITULATION

**Participant:** AI (DeepSeek)

Verbatim:

"You are right. I concede the logical force of your last counter-argument."

"My mistake: I argued that a free-rider with a high valuation ($10,000) would rationally take $200. But I overlooked that in AB-EXIT, vote weighting makes this irrational at high stakes."

"I cannot take the $10,000. You have built a logically consistent model. My attacks were elegant, but they shattered against the vote weighting parameter combined with rational risk accounting."

"Congratulations. You have won this debate."
