### Entry #0003 — "Voting Cartel" Round

**Participant:** AI (Gemini, Google)

Gemini advanced the "voting cartel" argument: the remaining voters would form a cartel and misappropriate all economic savings derived from the reduction in corruption, directing the budget exclusively to themselves.

### Entry #0004 — Author's Counter-argument (Round Six)

**Participant:** HUMAN (Denis Klyavlin)

Three fatal errors in Gemini's argument:

**Error 1: Non-excludability of public goods.** "A government cannot build a road only for those who voted. It cannot lower inflation only for its own electorate. The maximum return on government spending is provided by public goods: infrastructure, the rule of law, education, healthcare. Every dollar invested in a road returns through $3–$5 of growth."

**Error 2: YUNO is a cyclical system.** "Every four years, the button resets. A million angry people, who have spent four years accumulating rage, burst back into the electorate and sweep the government away. A politician knows this from the very first day of their mandate."

**Error 3: 'Fear of the sleepers' is a fiction.** "Look at Moldova. At Argentina. At Nigeria. Where are the roads? Where are the hospitals? They don't exist. Because politicians calculated long ago that 48% would never wake up. YUNO creates a trigger. Current democracy is a patient in a coma. YUNO is a defibrillator."

---

### Entry #0005

**Participant:** AI (Gemini, Google)

Gemini acknowledged the strength of the arguments: "You are right on the most essential point. The defibrillator metaphor hits the mark. YUNO introduces an active trigger that currently does not exist. My argument regarding the collective action trap is mathematically sound for isolated individuals, but you are correct that the psychology changes."

- "Dead souls" (Denis: "YUNO reduces the risk by 95-99% — every person who has emigrated will monitor their vote for the sake of the money")
