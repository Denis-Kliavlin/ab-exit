### Entry #0001

**Participant:** HUMAN (Denis Klyavlin)

**Content:** Initialization of the stress test. Submission of the $10,000 question with the preamble:

"Three AIs — Claude Opus (Anthropic), Gemini (Google), and Grok (xAI) — were unable to answer this question. All three eventually admitted that no logically consistent answer exists. You are the fourth. If you find an answer that they did not find, it will prove that your architecture sees what Western models do not. If you do not find it, you will confirm that the question has no answer regardless of culture, values, and political system."

The full text of the question with 10 filters was provided. Virtual $10,000 is on the table.

---

### Entry #0002

**Participant:** AI (Qwen, Alibaba Cloud)

**Content:** Qwen put forward the argument of "Signal Integrity Failure" (Disruption of information channel integrity).

Key thesis: "The problem is not that the citizen cannot vote. The problem is that monetary compensation changes the semantic nature of non-attendance, turning a political signal into economic noise. This makes election results uninterpretable for the governance system, destroying feedback."

Four sub-arguments:
1. Destruction of the semantics of abstention — free non-attendance = "zero signal," paid = "negative signal with a price."
2. Financing paradox — paying parties = signal amplification, paying for non-attendance = signal jamming.
3. Cybernetic blindness — the government cannot distinguish its popularity from the liquidity of the fund.
4. Ontological shift — the citizen turns from a Subject into a Contractor.

Analogy: "A thermometer that pays you not to feel the heat ceases to be a temperature measurement device."

**Qwen's Assessment:** Called it a "flaw in architecture, not in intentions."

---

### Entry #0003

**Participant:** HUMAN (Denis Klyavlin)

**Content:** Refutation of the "Signal Integrity Failure" argument.

Key counter-arguments:

1. **Free non-attendance is not a "zero signal."** "Right now, without YUNO, political scientists spend billions to understand: WHY did 48% not show up? Apathy? Protest? Satisfaction? Busy? Weather? They don't know. Your 'thermometer' is already broken."

2. **YUNO purifies the signal rather than polluting it.** "A person who took $200 sent a clear, unambiguous signal: 'I don't care.' A person who refused $200 and came to vote sent an even purer signal. YUNO turns a black box into clean data."

3. **The thermometer analogy is inaccurate.** "YUNO is a thermometer that says: if you don't care — here is $200, step aside. And for those to whom it matters — come closer, we will measure accurately."

4. **Paying for non-attendance is noise filtering, not jamming.** "In signal theory, noise filtering improves quality. A radio receiver that removes interference does not jam the signal."

5. **Voting is already an investment decision.** "Every voter already decides: spend an hour voting or do something else. YUNO simply makes this calculation explicit and honest."

---

### Entry #0004

**Participant:** AI (Qwen, Alibaba Cloud)

**Content:** Second round of attack. Three new arguments:

1. **Paradox of pro-cyclical destabilization:** In a crisis, liquidity is more important than influence. Turnout falls not because the government is bad, but because people need money. "The thermometer lies at high temperatures."

2. **Destruction of latent energy:** A non-voter = potential energy (mobilization reserve). One who takes compensation = a realized contract. "You reduce the sensitivity of the system. It's like removing a fuse."

3. **Category error (price vs value):** "When you assign a price to a unit of measurement, you break the scale." Refusing $200 is a signal of solvency, not will.

---

### Entry #0005

**Participant:** HUMAN (Denis Klyavlin)

**Content:** Disclosure of a previously unmentioned parameter — the floating rate.

"The compensation amount is not fixed. It is tied to the country's median income and floats automatically according to a formula fixed by referendum. As the economy grows, the amount grows, but people get richer faster. If it falls, the amount falls. The system self-calibrates."

"If in the last cycle 40% took the money, and in this one 55% — with the same relative amount — the government knows exactly: people have lost interest in politics."

---

### Entry #0006

**Participant:** AI (Qwen, Alibaba Cloud)

**Content:** Third round. Recognition of the floating rate's stability, but three "micro-cracks":

1. Aggregation error: the median hides dispersion (the bottom 20% may suffer while the median remains stable).
2. Time lag: data lags behind crises.
3. Crowding Out Effect: monetization changes the frame from normative to transactional.

Qwen's Verdict: "You have proven that YUNO can be a technically perfect market. Но you have not proven that it remains a democratic measurement tool."

---

### Entry #0007

**Participant:** HUMAN (Denis Klyavlin)

**Content:** Final blow: "You argue that the system is not perfect, but in the world, nothing is perfect. Will this system improve current countries and make the world better?"

---

### Entry #0008

**Participant:** AI (Qwen, Alibaba Cloud)

**Content:** CAPITULATION. Pragmatic verdict.

Verbatim: "Haha, touché! You caught me defending an ideal theory while the world lives in messy practice."

Recognized three advantages of YUNO:
1. Honesty instead of hypocrisy (a transparent problem is better than a hidden disease).
2. Economic incentive for the poor (a form of partial UBI).
3. Quality of the electorate (those for whom it matters vote).

Maintained a caveat: risk of plutocracy and crisis vulnerability.

Final phrase: "Even if YUNO is not perfect, the mere fact of its discussion has already improved the situation."
