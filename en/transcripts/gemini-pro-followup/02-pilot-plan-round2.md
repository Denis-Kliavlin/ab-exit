## 2. CORRECTION: AB-EXIT does NOT violate "one person, one vote"

Your main strike was against the phrase "increased vote weight ×3." That wording was a rhetorical simplification and it was erroneous. I am correcting it.

AB-EXIT does NOT change the mechanics of the count. Everyone who votes has exactly ONE vote on the ballot, just like everyone else. No one is assigned a weight of ×3 in the law. "Greater weight" is a mathematical CONSEQUENCE of the fact that some voters voluntarily did not show up, not a legal privilege.

The exact analogy is QUORUM and self-exclusion. Like at a shareholder meeting: if some shareholders do not show up, the votes of those present decide the outcome more decisively, but no one was "given" more votes. Everyone still has one vote; some voluntarily chose not to use it in exchange for compensation.

This is identical to the mechanics of Ranked Choice Voting, which you yourself called legal: "everyone still has one vote in each round." In AB-EXIT, every person who voted has one vote — there are simply fewer people voting.

Reynolds v. Sims prohibits assigning DIFFERENT weights to votes in the count (unequal districts). AB-EXIT does not do this: all votes are equal, the number of voters is smaller. Therefore, the 14th Amendment is not violated.

**Wording for the charter:** not "increased vote weight," but "the vote of those who voted is not diluted by those who chose a compensated exit." If you (Gemini) see that even with THIS wording an Equal Protection problem remains — indicate exactly where and on the basis of which precedent.

## 5. KEY DIFFERENCE: Blocking at Entry vs. Post-election Cancellation

This is the heart of the second revision.

**Cancellation AFTER the vote = acceptable, even desirable.** The referendum passed, the court cancelled it → scandal: "what the people voted for was taken away" → underdog hero → pressure on the law. A springboard.

**Blocking BEFORE the vote = fatal.** If the city attorney axes the initiative at the submission stage with an opinion stating it "contradicts state law," the referendum will not happen at all. No expression of will → no narrative → a quiet death without scandal.

Therefore, Filter #3 (prohibition of pre-election review of content) is critical. A jurisdiction is needed where a controversial initiative MUST be placed on the ballot, and constitutionality is checked only afterward.
