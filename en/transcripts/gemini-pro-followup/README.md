## Methodological Note

Round 3 (`03-hybrid-plan-round3.md`) contains an important methodological observation that extends beyond AB-EXIT and is applicable to any LLM-adversarial evaluation:

> "An LLM is STRONG in legal analysis (statutes, precedents, specific numbers—these are objective facts). And it is WEAK in strategic forecasts for situations without precedent: it projects past cases onto a new class of situations. This is a structural limitation of any LLM evaluation of the unprecedented."

This separation allowed for a candid **state-by-state legal verification** from the model (Oregon, California, Colorado, Massachusetts, Ohio) with specific citations of statutes, without mixing it with speculative forecasts of electoral success.

## Boundary of Reliability

The transcript is recorded **as reference material**, not as proof of the concept's truth.

— **Does not prove:** that AB-EXIT is guaranteed to work in the real world. LLM capitulation is not equal to empirical success.  
— **Proves:** that the concept has no obvious strategic vulnerabilities accessible to LLM-level analysis with access to political science data. The class of objections generated through political technology templates has been exhausted.  
— **Points to the next stage:** verifications inaccessible to LLMs are necessary (state election-law attorney, specialist in ballot initiatives, empirical sociologist).

Any future reader can read the transcript, evaluate the quality of Gemini Pro's argumentation, and draw their own conclusion as to which objections remain unaddressed.
