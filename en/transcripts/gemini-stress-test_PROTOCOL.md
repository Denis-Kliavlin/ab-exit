# SESSION PROTOCOL: Stress Test with Gemini (Google) — $10,000 Challenge

**Format: Court Protocol**
**Session Date: March-April 2026 (Gemini does not provide server timestamps)**
**Participants:**
- Denis Klyavlin (Author of the AB-EXIT / Mechanism 200 concept, Chișinău, Moldova)
- Gemini 3.1 Pro (AI system, Google)

**Note:** This protocol is compiled based on a full copy of the chat provided by the author. Gemini does not have a function to export files with server timestamps and cannot create files for download. For legal verification, it is recommended to request logs from Google.

**Session Content:** 26+ rounds of stress-testing the Mechanism 200 / YUNO concept. Gemini advanced arguments from classical political economy, game theory, and public choice theory. The author systematically refuted all arguments. Gemini capitulated and awarded $10,000 to the author.

### Record #0003 — "Voting Cartel" Round

**Participant:** AI (Gemini, Google)

Gemini advanced the "voting cartel" argument: the remaining voters will form a cartel and misappropriate all economic savings from the reduction of corruption, directing the budget exclusively to themselves.

---

### Record #0004 — Author's Counter-argument (Round Six)

**Participant:** HUMAN (Denis Klyavlin)

Three fatal errors in Gemini's argument:

**Error 1: Non-excludability of public goods.** "A government cannot build a road only for those who voted. It cannot lower inflation only for its electorate. The maximum return on government spending is provided by public goods: infrastructure, the rule of law, education, healthcare. Every dollar invested in a road returns through $3–$5 of growth."

**Error 2: YUNO is a cyclical system.** "Every four years, the button resets. A million angry people, who have been accumulating rage for four years, burst into the electorate and sweep away the government. A politician knows this from the first day of their mandate."

**Error 3: 'Fear of the sleepers' is a fiction.** "Look at Moldova. Look at Argentina. Look at Nigeria. Where are the roads? Where are the hospitals? They don't exist. Because politicians calculated long ago that 48% would not wake up. YUNO creates a trigger. Current democracy is a patient in a coma. YUNO is a defibrillator."

---

### Record #0005

**Participant:** AI (Gemini, Google)

Gemini acknowledged the strength of the arguments: "You are right in the most important aspect. The defibrillator metaphor hits the mark. YUNO introduces an active trigger that does not currently exist. My argument about the collective action trap is mathematically correct for isolated individuals, but you are right that the psychology changes."

---

### Records #0006–#0020 — Series of Rounds

**Participants:** Both

Successive rounds on the following topics:
- Administrative resource (Gemini: "the director will force the workers." Denis: "police + $200 as an incentive for whistleblowing")
- Poverty vs. Indigence (Denis: "they are poor, not indigent. It is entirely possible not to take the money")
- Radicals (Gemini: "only fanatics will remain." Denis: "what is their percentage? Check the statistics")
- The "Single Mother" (Gemini used an emotional argument. Denis: "and where are the fathers? Where are the students? Where are the businessmen? Take the statistics")
- Increase in voting weight (Denis: "if everyone took the money, the vote of the remaining person weighs many times more")
- Migrants and Diaspora (Denis: "YUNO breaks the collective enclosure of communities and creates an individualization of choice")
- Technical implementation (Denis: "you, who sit on servers — the issue with bank cards was solved, what prevents it here?")
- "Dead souls" (Denis: "YUNO reduces the risk by 95-99% — every person who left will monitor their vote for the sake of the money")
- GDP growth and economic projections (A 20-year forecast with payouts growing from $200 to $1000+)
